    📖 What each line means

    • SALARY — your basic contracted monthly pay.
    • LEISUR CLB UPSEL — commission for leisure-club memberships you upsold to guests.
    • HRS-O/TIME — overtime: extra hours paid at your hourly rate (units × rate).
    • PAY-BACK PAY — back pay owed from an earlier period (e.g. a backdated rise).
    • PAY-TMOTM — a one-off award payment (e.g. "Team Member of the Month").
    • PAY HOLS & LIEU — payment for holiday or time-off-in-lieu hours.
    • HOLIDAYS TAKEN — paid holiday days taken, at the day rate (£127.11 = 10h × £12.71).
    • LIEU DAYS TAKEN — time-off-in-lieu days taken, at the same day rate.
    • ENHANCE HOL RATE — holiday-pay top-up so leave is paid at your average rate (incl. regular extras), as UK law requires — not just basic.
    • DYS-UNPAID SICK — a deduction for an unpaid sick day (shows as a minus).
    • DAYS CO SICK PAY — company sick pay: paid sick days at the day rate (units × £127.11 = 10h × £12.71).
    • NOR1 — an additional pay element/allowance; if unsure what it covers, ask payroll.
    • OTHERS — a year-to-date bucket totalling smaller miscellaneous items.
    • TAX — PAYE income tax. Your code 1257L means £12,570/yr is tax-free.
    • NAT INSURANCE — your National Insurance (Category A).
    • WPR NEST PEN 1 — your workplace pension paid into NEST.
    • HSF — a voluntary health cash-plan deduction (commonly the "Hospital Saturday Fund"); check with HR if unsure what it covers.
    • Employer NIC / Employer Pens — paid by your employer on top of your wage; not taken from you.
    • NET PAY (BANK) — what actually lands in your bank account.
